Documentation

Endpoints

Our API offers three main endpoints, each designed to provide you with specific sets of job information. Please review the following endpoints:

'/api/jobs'

The '/api/jobs' endpoint allows you to retrieve a list of jobs across various categories and experience levels. This endpoint is perfect for obtaining a broad overview of the available job opportunities. Explore the rich diversity of job data provided by our API to enhance the content on your job board websites or for testing purposes. 

Example:
                        
GET /api/jobs

'/api/jobs/:category'

With the '/api/jobs/:category endpoint', you can filter job data based on specific categories or industries. Tailor your requests to focus on a particular job sector, providing more targeted and relevant results for your users.

Parameters:

:category (String): The job category or industry you want to retrieve jobs for. 

Example:
                        
GET /api/jobs/technology

'/api/jobs/:category/:amount'

The '/api/jobs/:category/:amount' endpoint allows you to control the number of jobs returned for a specific category. This flexibility enables you to fine-tune the amount of data received, optimizing the performance of your applications.

Parameters:

:category (String): The job category or industry you want to retrieve jobs for.

:amount (Number): The desired number of jobs to be returned. 

Example:
                        
GET /api/jobs/technology/10

Parameters

When interacting with our API, you can customize your requests using parameters to refine the job data you receive. Explore the following parameters to tailor your queries and obtain job information that best fits your needs. Whether you're filtering by category, controlling the number of results, or searching for specific job titles, these parameters provide the flexibility to enhance your development experience.

Categories

The categories parameter allows you to narrow down job results based on specific industries or categories. By specifying a category in your request, you can focus on retrieving job data that aligns with particular sectors of interest.

Amount

The amount parameter enables you to control the quantity of job results returned in a single request. Fine-tune your requests by specifying the number of jobs you want to receive, providing flexibility in managing the volume of data for your application.

Query

The query parameter, extracted from the query string, allows you to perform targeted searches within job titles. Use this parameter to filter results and retrieve only those jobs whose titles contain the specified query string.

This parameter is particularly useful when you have specific job titles in mind and want to narrow down the results to match your search criteria. 

Example:
                        
GET /api/jobs/technology/10?query=developer
